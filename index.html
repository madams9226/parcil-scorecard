What are our lead measures?
Lead Measure 01
Conversion Experiment Tests
Structured tests to improve how well our listings and pages turn visitors into buyers.
Examples
  • Main product image tests
  • A+ content variations
  • Product detail page (PDP) layout tests
  • Secondary image tests
  • Title and bullet point tests
Lead Measure 02
Promotional Offers + Discounts
Creating and launching promotions that give customers a compelling reason to buy now.
Examples
  • Product-specific discount offers
  • Sitewide sale events
  • Company-wide promotional campaigns
  • Limited-time bundle deals
Lead Measure 03
Merchandising Tests
Testing how we present and organize products so customers can discover and buy more easily.
Examples
  • Website navigation changes
  • Product placement and ordering
  • Website design and layout tests
  • Amazon parent/child relationship tests

Pull your total orders from both channels, add them together. Then add the total sessions from both channels. Divide orders by sessions.

  2. Get Amazon orders from Seller Central
  3. Add them: Total Orders
  4. Repeat for sessions from both platforms
  5. Divide Total Orders ÷ Total Sessions
Revenue per Session
↓ Lower is better
Total Revenue (all channels)
÷
Total Sessions (all channels)
A lower cost per session means you're generating more revenue relative to traffic. Rising costs signal inefficiency.
  1. Add revenue from website + Amazon
  2. Add sessions from website + Amazon
  3. Divide Revenue ÷ Sessions
  4. Track: if this number goes up vs target, that's red

▶ How to calculate these numbers
Amazon Unit Session %
↑ Higher is better
Units Sold (Amazon)
÷
Total Sessions (Amazon)
Found directly in Amazon Seller Central under Business Reports. This is Amazon's native metric — you can pull it without manual calculation.
  1. Go to Seller Central → Reports → Business Reports
  2. Find "Unit Session Percentage" column
  3. Use the period average for the month
Website CVR
↑ Higher is better
Website Orders
÷
Website Sessions
Pull from your ecommerce analytics platform (Shopify, GA4, etc.). Orders ÷ Sessions for the period.
  1. Get total orders from your website platform
  2. Get total sessions from analytics
  3. Divide: Orders ÷ Sessions × 100 for %
Blended AOV
↑ Higher is better
Total Revenue (all channels)
÷
Total Orders (all channels)
Add up revenue from all channels, divide by total number of orders from all channels. Higher AOV means more value per transaction.
  1. Sum revenue: website + Amazon + any other channel
  2. Sum orders: website + Amazon + any other channel
  3. Divide: Total Revenue ÷ Total Orders
